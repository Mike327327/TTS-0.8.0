TTS Personalization

This page shows the results of the Czech TTS models. First, results of base models are presented along with examples. This is followed by example of non-fine-tuned and fine-tuned samples (male and female speakers). Each section is briefly introduced and the results are commented.

Base models

1M and 200k

The 1M model is capable of generating shorter samples conditioned on d-vectors in a decent quality (samples B1 and B2). However, longer inputs caused issues and speech was unintelligible (sample B3). This issue was solved by segmenting the input text based on commas (sample B4). The 200k model performed worse than the 1M model. Segmenting the input text sometimes did not help and speech was not intelligible, as can be heard in sample B5.

Male personalization

1M, 1M(P), and 200k(P20)

The generated examples below are compared to the ground truth (first column). The following three samples on each line were generated using the 1M, 1M(P), and 200k(P20) models. Especially interesting is the comparison of the 1M(P) and 200k(P20) models — the 1M(P) was fine-tuned for 70k steps using 70 minutes of the target speaker training samples, whereas the 200k(P20) model used only 10 minutes and ran for 20k training steps. Both 1M(P) and 200k(P) produced almost identical results, therefore, comparing them to each other is fair.
